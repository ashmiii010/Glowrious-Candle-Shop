My Features

Here are some of the features I have acquired over the years

  • Body Shape: Pigs have a stout, bulky body with short legs.
  • Skin: Their skin is thick and covered with a coat of stiff bristles, which can vary in color among different breeds.
  • Snout: They have a long snout with a flat, rounded disk at the end, which they use to root and search for food
  • Senses: Pigs have a good sense of smell and hearing, but their eyesight is poor.
  • Hooves: Their hooves are cloven, meaning they are split into two.
  • Tusks: Wild pigs have sharp tusks used for digging and defense, while domestic pigs have tusklike teeth.
  • Size: Domestic pigs can weigh up to 700 pounds, while wild boars are smaller.
